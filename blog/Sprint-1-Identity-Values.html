Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

Once while driving around Wellington with the family, we stopped at some red lights near the Basin Reserve and there was a man standing/falling about on the left-hand lane. I think I might have usually left him and considered it not my problem. Instead, I decided that it was problem, and something needed to be done about it, so as the lights were turning green I got out of the car and took him off the road to the foot path and called the emergency services to get him some help. Looking back now I would like to think I would do the same today.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

I’d say that my core values are likely to be the same as most people, but growing up around Londoners really taught me to value being able to have a laugh and take the piss out of yourself.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

Luckily for me I spent a small fortune on a skills and strengths test a few months ago so that I could have my own official psychological astrology chart! I joke, somewhat… I enjoy analysis and analytics, breaking things down and finding out how they work. I am a hands-on learner; I prefer to explore things in an iterative manner first and then apply related abstractions after. I also enjoy working in a team environment, where each member is supportive and coordinated, yet allowed to work towards their own strengths and goals with the ultimate goal being the uplifting of everyone.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

Understanding the importance and nature of time in relation to both work and learning has been a source of disruption. I have often been lacking in time-management and thus have failed to set realistic goals and parameters to projects and assignments.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

I’ve have often found that when a new project arises there is often a clash of egos as members of a team begin considering and digesting the possible and probably issues and outcomes of one or another approach. I have certainly been one of those egos, especially when I was younger. As I have matured (hopefully) though I have realised that the real joy of teamwork isn’t in having everyone agree with you, rather it is in facilitating the abilities of others. Rather than push back on a coworker’s ideas I now instead help them explore and execute them and focus instead on coordination and lending a hand where needed.

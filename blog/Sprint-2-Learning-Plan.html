A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

Like a lot of people, I can get a little frustrated and annoyed when I feel like I’m banging my head against a proverbial brick wall which can lead to too much self-criticism. I am also not the greatest manager of time due to things like “perfectionism” and unrealistic planning. These past two weeks I have been using the suggested tools to really make every minute mean something by using the Sprint time-boxes, a pomodoro planner and planner. I think with some continued consistency my time management can be flipped from a weakness to a strength. Likewise, by learning to break down problems into discreet parts, I think I can successfully overcome that brick wall. I learn best when the experience is somewhat iterative and I don’t mind jumping into new subject areas and seeing how things work (or don’t). I believe I also possess curiosity and a passion for know how things work; both of which allow me to seek out ways of reformulating new and complex ideas into a more familiar form.

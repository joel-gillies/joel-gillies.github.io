What is your long-term goal or career pathway?

I am hoping that learning to code might open new life opportunities, that learning how to code can help augment my current repertoire of technical and interpersonal skills and help me grow my professional and personal potential. Having previously worked in government, I can see a path for me in the development of public goods and services web applications. Project planning and management has also been something I have had experience with, in pursuing a career within Full Stack development I might be able to apply this skill again. Likewise, I have enjoyed writing technical manuals in the past, most often for general audiences with no prior knowledge of the subject matter, it has been something I have always received positive feedback on, and I hope to be able develop and share this skill in the future.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

I think my biggest strength is my outward response to pressure and negative outcomes, I believe I bring a strong set of interpersonal skills to the table and a "no factor" kind of attitude to the team environment. That is, I always believe the project is achievable and that technical and material deficiencies can usually be overcome so long as the team is cohesive. I am afraid that biggest limitation is a technical one, that this will inevitably lead sustained periods of confusion about the syntax and the internal logic of the coding languages we will be using.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

Staying on top of the material is my major concern, as I have already experienced certain kind of lag between myself and others during Foundations. Prolonged instances of confusion can lead to increasing instances of directionless thinking, which in turn produce periods of self-doubt.

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would like to get better at admitting a lack of understanding and knowledge, in the past when I have worked in high stress environments a lack of skill or expertise has often led to negative feedback loops within the workplace. I will need to overcome my concern that such behaviours will reoccur and ensure that I keep my teammates apprised of the situation. I would also like to improve my leadership skills; project management is something I would like to undertake in the future and the accountability and organisation required of leadership is something I would like to develop.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme

I believe I currently work well with others and am capable of fitting into a fluid learning environment. To manage my workload I have already begun developing micro-time management skills via the use of timers and digital aids. When things get difficult I will ensure that I continue to communicate with the team and my facilitators. I will also make sure I go through appropriate troubleshooting, using digital resources to try and fix a problem. However if the problem persists I will reach out and seek help from others.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

As explained above, I recognise the importance of ensuring ongoing communication with my team and my facilitators when I have exhausted my own efforts at troubleshooting. I will use the tech-questions and cohort Discord servers, if I need further assistance I will contact my facilitator directly via dm.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

Ideally, I would hope for them to be amenable and affable in the face of my tiring ignorance (lol), within reason. I'd also hope for a sustained level of mutual respect for myself and my colleagues, and that if any problems arise, they will be dealt with in a professional manner.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

Given the hybrid nature of the course I will first attempt to reach out to our facilitators over discord, either via DM or in a general Discord server, before getting in touch with onsite staff if an issue persists.

My commitment

I will endeavour to keep up with the workload as best as possible, on the understanding that falling behind in a collaborative environment affects everyone else just as much as it affects me. Therefore, when I feel that I am unable to continue my technical progress alone I will reach out for help straight away and let my fellow students know what the situation is. I'll look to bring my professionalism to Boot Camp every day and will treat it and others with the respect due to a workplace. I do not expect everyone to be at their best day in, day out and if required will happily lend a hand to anyone who needs it. Likewise, I will make sure that I respect my own boundaries and capabilities, on the understanding that I can't work ceaselessly without time away from the workspace.
